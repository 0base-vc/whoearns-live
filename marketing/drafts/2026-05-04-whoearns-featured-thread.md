## Thread

### 1

WhoEarns is an open dashboard for Solana validator income.

It shows what validators earned from leader slots: block fees, on-chain Jito tips, produced slots, skipped slots, and performance per scheduled slot.

https://whoearns.live

### 2

Validator income is not one number.

A validator can earn more because it had more scheduled blocks, fewer skips, stronger priority-fee capture, or more on-chain Jito tips.

WhoEarns separates those pieces so the data can be inspected.

### 3

For validators:

WhoEarns is a public performance page for your operator work.

Search your vote or identity pubkey, inspect closed-epoch results, compare against peers, and claim your profile so stakers can find the right operator identity.

### 4

For stakers:

Commission and uptime are not the whole story.

WhoEarns helps you compare how validators performed when they were scheduled to lead: produced slots, skip rate, block fees, and tips.

Data first. Delegation choices still need judgment.

### 5

Why the default ranking is performance:

(block fees + on-chain Jito tips) / scheduled slots

Raw total income is stake-biased. Bigger validators usually get more leader slots.

Performance is closer to an operator-efficiency view.

### 6

Closed epochs are final. Live epochs are provisional.

WhoEarns keeps those separate so rankings are not mixed with still-changing current-epoch data.

Use closed epochs for recaps. Use live data as "so far" context.
